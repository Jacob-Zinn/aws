I'm really not about to write a whole story, but a few days ago a friend suggested that we both write poems and have them judged. Judge?




Let’s play a game
It won’t be taboo
We’ve been playing games
Since we were two

Oh the games we used to play
The thrills of peek-a-boo
I could lie and say I envy those times
But I would rather be true

The games only got better
As the years flew by
We learned to play tag
and I spy with our little eyes

So back to the game
I told you we’d play
The best of them all
The one we’re already playing

A game full of tomorrows
Full of love and equal parts sorrow
Full of dreams and disappointment
pride and shame

It ends only in time
Every second another chance
A chance to do good
A chance to progress

There are no real winners or losers
Though we can name a few greats
The ones who made the world better
But not for the people’s praise

There was one who once screamed
“I have a dream”
And he died a martyr
But with an unforgotten legacy

Curiosity provokes the outstanding question:
How to achieve a desirable fate….
A life one can look back on while dying
And finding oneself in a blissful state

Your guess is as good as mine
Maybe we try to leave something behind

A mark on the world
A mark that will stand the tests of time
It’s like planting a tree
It’s working towards something you won’t get to see

Maybe it’s a simple act
Loving words to a wounded friend
Mending their heart
And providing courage to try again

Maybe our only task
Is to answer the door
When opportunity knocks
Before it is lost forevermore

However you play
It’s best not to forget
Playing games has always been fun
And this one’s no different
Words can’t explain
What a heart longs to tell
The secrets of young, pretty girls

She’s covered in youth
So what could she give?
How sudden, you’re lost in her curls.

And then there’s the boy.
That beautiful boy.
Who thinks he’s becoming a man

But when I look upon
The face I adore
I see only a lost Peter Pan.

So tell me dear Peter
if we were an island
how long would you ask me to stay?

would I be that Wendy?
your first in command
fighting pirates, and flying away

Or am I the Belle
who’s wings only break
Each time her poor heart understands

They’ll never be more
Than convenient, old friends
And she’ll never win love from that Pan.

To be an “adult”
They say that it means
To put childhood away on the shelf

What if being grown up
Isn’t being eighteen
Or having material wealth

Away with the plans
With all of the dreams
The things I’ve been desperate to do

I don’t want to be big
No, I’d rather be small.
And stay on the island with you.
